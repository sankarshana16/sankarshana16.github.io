Research Focus

My work focuses on testing gravity on cosmological scales using large-scale structure observables, combining theory, simulations, and data analysis to build robust inference pipelines capable of probing deviations from General Relativity across cosmic time and scale.

Long-Term Research Program

My long-term goal is to build fully nonlinear cosmological inference frameworks that enable model-agnostic tests of gravity using upcoming precision surveys. This includes combining nonlinear modified gravity modelling, Gaussian process emulation of simulations, Bayesian inference, and machine learning–driven simulation-based inference.

A central component of this effort is the Cosmological Gravity on All Scales series, where I developed a model-agnostic parameterization of gravity using redshift-binned effective gravitational strength and gravitational slip, alongside nonlinear emulators and full MCMC pipelines for large-scale structure and CMB lensing analyses.
