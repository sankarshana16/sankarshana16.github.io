Research Program

My long-term research program focuses on building fully nonlinear cosmological inference frameworks that enable model-agnostic tests of gravity using surveys such as Euclid and the Vera C. Rubin Observatory Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST).

This includes combining nonlinear modified gravity modelling, Gaussian process emulation, Bayesian inference pipelines, and simulation-based inference across multiple probes including cosmic shear, galaxy clustering, CMB lensing, and spectroscopic observables.

A central part of this effort is the Cosmological Gravity on All Scales series, where I developed a model-agnostic parameterization of gravity using redshift-binned effective gravitational strength and gravitational slip, alongside nonlinear emulators and full MCMC pipelines for large-scale structure and CMB lensing analyses.
